History...

I bought this car in 1986 from the local Nissan dealer, who had sold it new and taken it back from the original owner as a trade-in, with just under 60,000 miles on it. For the next 2 years it was my primary vehicle, so it spent some time on New York winter highways. But it wasn't long before I needed a family car and it became a rarely-driven summer fun-mobile. By the fall of 1994 we couldn't afford to keep 3 cars on the road so I drove the car into the barn (not a 'working' barn -- no hay or animals), pulled the plates, and put a tarp over it intending to put it back on the road the following year when financial conditions improved.

Well, one thing led to another and I never did get it back on the road. So it sat there until a few months ago when we had to do some work in the barn and the car needed to be moved. We pushed it out of the barn and I had a mechanic give me an estimate to fix up the body and get it running again. I'm no car expert so I can't vouch for what he said, but he thought the frame and the drivetrain were in very good shape and it would be a great ride again with some TLC. He said that he thought it would be a terrible shame to just junk it or sell it for parts. We thought so too, so it's been sitting in the driveway in a car bag while my wife and I tried to decide whether to fix it or not (we really love the car) and we finally decided that we just had to sell it.

Interior (the best part)...
The interior of the car is in really great shape.  
There's a small crack in the dash vinyl, right in front of the steering wheel from spending a bit too much time in the summer sun.  
The seats are tan cloth and have no tears or even visible wear. 
The floors were always protected by mats, so they look great, and the floor itself is solid with no sign of weakness.  
The carpet behind the seats is all intact and very clean, as is the ceiling. 

The Body (the worst part)...
There are a few rust spots on almost every part of the exterior, but most of them can be cleaned up, at least according to my neighbor who runs a body shop.  


The doors, rocker panels, and fenders are all pretty rusty and will need to be replaced or seriously repaired.  


The car had an underbody coating applied so there is little or no rust on the frame that I can see (I tried to take some pictures) but the car hasn't been up on a lift in years so all I know is what I and my mechanic can see crawling around. The tires are 15 years old, pretty rough and cracked, but will hold air at least for a few days. 
All of the glass and chrome is in great shape! 

Drivetrain (Does it run?)...
I wish I could say that it runs great but I really have no idea. The big inline 6 was running great when I drove it into the barn! Most of the miles on the car are easy highway miles according to both me and the previous owner. It was always well-maintained -- oil changes every 3k miles and routine scheduled maintenance -- and I have some of the maintenance records. I didn't do anything really to store it properly, since I intended to put it back on the road in a few months, so I haven't tried to get it running in any way -- I'm afraid that I might cause some serious harm to the engine if I didn't do all the right things after letting it sit for so long. I also haven't put a new battery in it, so I don't know what the condition of the electrical system is like, although it looks fine. The parking brake works, it has a brand new clutch with something like 30 miles on it, but the clutch cable seems to be broken.

Bottom line...

If you can work on engines and do some body work, and if you can invest a bit of time, this car would make a great restoration project. You could end up with a classic car that's worth as much as $10,000 or more -- the 1981 280Z was almost the last of the classic Datsun Z cars before Nissan came out with the heavier, fancier, rebranded 300Z series in 1983. That's what we took into consideration when we thought about fixing it up ourselves. But I just don't have the time or the knowledge to do the work myself and, well we just don't have the money to pay someone else to do it.
